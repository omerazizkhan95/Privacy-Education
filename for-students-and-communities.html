Digitally fluent. Rights illiterate.

Today's youth can navigate any app, platform, or device with ease. But most cannot identify how their personal data is collected, sold, or weaponized against them. Underrepresented women face a compounded disadvantage — they are both the least informed and the most exploited. privacy.education exists to close that gap, permanently.

Who we serve

Built for those the
digital world overlooked.

Two audiences. One shared right — to understand and control their digital lives.

🎒

K-12 Students

From primary school to high school, students interact with data-harvesting platforms every day. We meet them where they are — with age-appropriate, curriculum-aligned learning that transforms how they see and interact with the digital world.

👩🏽‍💻

Underrepresented Women

Women from marginalized communities face the highest rates of algorithmic harm and digital exclusion. Our content is culturally affirming, accessible, and designed to build confidence and fluency for communities historically left out of tech education.

What we teach

Practical fluency.
Not just awareness.

Six learning pillars that build real-world digital literacy from the ground up.

🔐

Data Privacy Fundamentals

What personal data is, how it is collected, and why it matters — in plain language that resonates with students at every level.

🧠

Algorithmic Awareness

How recommendation engines and AI systems shape what we see and feel — and how to recognize when they are working against us.

🛡️

Digital Rights & Consent

What rights individuals hold over their data, what consent means online, and how to exercise those rights with confidence.

👁️

Manipulative Design Patterns

How to identify dark patterns and deceptive UX tactics designed to extract data and attention without informed consent.

🗂️

Digital Footprint Auditing

Practical tools and guided exercises to help learners map and take control of the digital trail they leave across platforms.

💬

Online Safety & Wellbeing

The connection between algorithmic exposure, mental health, and self-worth — and how awareness becomes a protective factor.

How it works

A learning journey built
for real lives.

Four stages that take learners from awareness to action.

Step 01

Discover your digital world

Learners explore how data flows through their everyday digital lives through interactive modules that make the invisible visible.

Step 02

Understand your rights

Learners explore the legal and ethical frameworks that govern their data under laws like PIPEDA, GDPR, and COPPA.

Step 03

Identify the risks

From algorithmic manipulation to data breaches, learners develop the critical eye to spot when technology is being used against them.

Step 04

Take control

Learners leave with a concrete personal action plan — tools, habits, and the confidence to make informed choices going forward.
